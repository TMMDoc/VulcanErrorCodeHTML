Centering the beam to the nozzle (Tapeshot)

  • The service user level is activated. Means, Tools, Materials

  • Measuring magnifier (10 to 20 fold magnification).

  • Adhesive tape (transparent Tesa Film).

  • Permanent felt-tip marker (for example, Edding 400).

  • Allen key 4 mm AF.

  • Allen key 3 mm AF.

    1. Move the cutting unit to the front.

    2. Attach a nozzle (EAA10) to the nozzle holder.

    3. Mark directly in front of the nozzle. .Fig. 3‑19

      |

  1. Stick adhesive strips without tension over the nozzle orifice.

    • Do not touch the adhesive strips in the scorching areas toprevent impurities. .Fig. 3‑20

  2. Click “Nozzle center”. .Fig. 3‑21

    |

  1. Go with setting:

    • Focus diameter: 150 µm.

    • Focus position: 0 mm.

    • Power: 1000 Watts.

    • Duration: 0.001 s.

    • Need Vulcan service user level to modify the parameters.

    • If the adhesive strip isn’t burnt through, increase the “Dwell time” appropriately. .Fig. 3‑22

  1. Close the safety door and lock it.

  2. Press the START button.

  5. Check that the hole in the adhesive strip is centered in the nozzle orifice with the aid of measuring magnifier. .Fig. 3‑23

    |

  1. If the hole in the adhesive strip is not centered in the nozzle orifice, use the mark made on the nozzle to determine the direction in which the adjustment must be made.

Determining the focal position

The machine is not equipped with “BrightLine fiber” and BM111 has only one focal diameter. With KL59, two focus combs with different focal diameters are cut. With BM111, only one focus comb is cut. Difference with different cutting unit Conditions * The protective glass has been checked and replaced, if nec-essary (see “Checking and cleaning protective glass (KL59)”,pg. xxx). * Nozzle centering has been checked and set, if necessary (see “Centering the beam to the nozzle (Tapeshot)”, pg. xxx). * The service user level is active. Means, Tools, Materials * Mild or stainless-steel sheet, sheet thickness 1 mm. * Feeler gauge.

  1. Select “Controls” in HMI. Cutting focusing comb

  2. Press “Focus Comb”. .Fig. 3‑26

    |

  1. Select 150µm for the “Focus Diameter”.

    • With KL59, another focus comb of focus diameter 210µm needs to cut later.

    • With BM111, select “EFocusDiameter.TwoHundred” for the “Focus Diameter”.

    • The technology will be chosen automatically according to the focus diameter. .Fig. 3‑27

      |

  1. Apply the appropriate nozzle according to the technology.

    • KL59: EAA23.

    • BM111: size 2.0.

  2. Place a 1 mm thick sheet on the pallet (according to the technology).

  3. Select “Setup” > “work offset” to set work offset:

    • “Positioning diode” to start cutting the reference part at the positioning diode.

    • “Nozzle center” to start cutting the reference part at the center of the nozzle. .Fig. 3‑28

      |

  1. Back to the program “Focus comb”.

  2. Close the safety door and lock it.

  3. Make sure the laser is standby.

    • Standby button on control panel is illuminated.

    • Laser icon in the bottom-right corner of HMI is active. .Fig. 3‑29

      |

  1. Press the START button. The focus comb will be cut.

  2. Repeat the above step to cut the focus comb with focal diameter 210 µm.

  3. Determine the smallest kerf “K” (= focus position) in the focuscomb: Determining the focus position and entering offset

    • With the suitable feeler gauge (e.g., 0.25 mm), starting from the left and right, respectively, check every individual kerf.

    • Mark the kerf where the feeler gauge sticks on both left and right sides. .Fig. 3‑30

  1. Calculate the focus shift and enter it under “Focus Shift1”. Example: Old focus shift A mm, smallest kerf K mm. Focus shift = A + K. .Fig. 3‑31

Scratches in the protective glass due to soiled swabs. * Insert the swab in the hole in the cleaning fixture that is intended for this purpose. * Dispose of the swab after use.

  1. Drip methanol on to the swab. Shake out excess methanol. .Drip methanol over the swab Fig. 3‑38

    |

  1. Remove coarse dirt by rubbing with the moistened swab. .Cleaning the protective glass with the swab Fig. 3‑39

    |

| a|

Protective glass contaminated by deposits. * Do not deposit swabs on the protective glass.

  1. Remove fine contamination with the flat side of the swab by evenly and slowly drawing it across the surface from one edge to the other.

  2. Wipe the swab slowly over the protective glass once more to remove the methanol haze.

  3. Turn the protective glass over and clean the rear side as described above.

  4. Clean the sealing surface, the inside and outside of the cartridge with the swab while it is still wet.

    • The swab may not be used to clean the protective glass again.

  5. Dispose of the swab.

  6. Insert the protective glass into the cartridge. Make sure that the O-ring the protective glass rest on is present and not damaged. | a|

Damage to the protective glass possible. * Make sure that the protective glass does not collide with the housing of the cutting head.

  1. Insert the cartridge and tighten the knurled nut with the hook wrench, until the arrow is exactly below the lock symbol. .Arrow marking in the cutting unit Fig. 3‑40

    | |

|1- Lock symbol |2- Arrow

  1. If the contamination cannot be completely removed, replace the straight protective glass.
