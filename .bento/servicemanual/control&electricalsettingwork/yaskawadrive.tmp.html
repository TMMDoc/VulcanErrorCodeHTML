Replacing Yaskawa drive

  1. Switch off the machine.

  2. Disconnect all the cables from the Yaskawa drive. .Fig. 5‑87

    |

  1. Remove the Yaskawa drive from the switch cabinet.

  2. Mount a new Yaskawa drive.

  3. Connect your laptop to CN7 port on the drive by Mini USB cable.

    • The driver needs to install when connecting for the first time. .Fig. 5‑88

      |

  1. Open the software SigmaWin+.

  2. Select “Start” > “Connect the SERVOPACK”. .Fig. 5‑89

    |

  1. Select “USB Connection” > “Search for SERVOPACKs”. .Fig. 5‑90

    |

  1. Select the target drive and connect.

  2. Click on menu button in the top-left corner of the interface. .Fig. 5‑91

    |

  1. Select “I/O Signal Allocation” for the pop-up window. .Fig. 5‑92

    |

  1. Allocate the pin number for input signal of prohibiting the for-ward/reverse run and press “Write”.

    • P-OT: CN1-7.

    • N-OT: CN1-8. .Fig. 5‑93

      |

  1. Acknowledge the warning.

  2. Select “Jog” in main menu. .Fig. 5‑94

    |

  1. Acknowledge the warning to enter the jog operation.

  2. Make sure the servo is on. .Fig. 5‑95

    |

  1. Click the forward/reverse button several times to approach the upper/lower limit position and check if the “P-OT”/ “N-OT”signal turns green. .Fig. 5‑96

    |

  1. Check that the pointer can reach both upper and lower limit position and the alarm “A.710” is triggered. .Upper limit position Fig. 5‑97

    | |

  1. Switch off the servo by the “Servo OFF” button.

  2. Double click the Axis interface (orange area) to show alarm Display.

  3. Reset the alarm by click “Reset axes” button. .Fig. 5‑98

    |

  1. Drive the servo motor on

  2. Move the focus position away from the upper/lower limit position.

  3. Restart the machine and then the servo motor will do homing automatically.
