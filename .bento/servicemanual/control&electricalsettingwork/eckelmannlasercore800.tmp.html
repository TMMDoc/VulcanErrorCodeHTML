Replacing the Eckelmann controller

  • A flat-bladed screwdriver. Disassemble the controller

    | a|

Hazardous voltage! Risk of electric shock! * All connectors may only be connected and disconnected when power is off.

  1. Switch off the main switch.

  2. Disconnect all the cables from the controller. .Fig. 5‑34

    |

  1. Pull out the two retaining springs with a flat-bladed screwdriver until they click into place. .Fig. 5‑35

    |

  1. Remove the device from the DIN rail by tilting it upwards. .Fig. 5‑36

    |

  1. Using a flat-bladed screwdriver, pull out the two retaining springs on the underside of the new controller until they click into place. Assemble the controller .Fig. 5‑37

    |

  1. Place the controller on the top edge of the DIN rail and tilt it downwards until the controller is positioned on the lower edge of the DIN rail. .Fig. 5‑38

    |

  1. Press the two retaining springs until they click into place. .Fig. 5‑39

    |

  1. Check that the controller is firmly attached to the DIN rail.

  2. Connect all the cables to the controller according to the label.

  3. Switch on the machine.

  4. Establish connection with Eckelmann controller (see “Establi-shing connection with Eckelmann controller”, pg. xxx). Further work

  5. Go to HMI, run the program “Nozzle clean & height sensor calibration”.

Replacing the battery of Eckelmann controller

Life of a battery depends on the operational time and ambient temperature. It is recommended to exchange the battery after 5 years.

| a|

Battery mistreated. Risk of explosion! * Do not recharge, disassemble, or dispose of battery in fire. * The battery replacement may only be performed by qualified person. * If the battery exchange is completed within 15 minutes (time between power off and the mounting of a new battery), data loss in the RAM can be avoided.

  1. Switch off the main switch.

  2. Remove the controller from the DIN rail (see “Replacing the Eckelmann controller”, pg. xxx).

  3. Push the plastic lid’s two left-hand snap lugs inwards and upwards with a flat-bladed screwdriver. .Fig. 5‑49

    |

  1. Lift the lid with a swinging movement. .Fig. 5‑50

    |

  1. Pull the battery up out of its holder.

    • Please dispose of the battery according to the local regulation. .Fig. 5‑51

      |

  1. Touch the new battery (Type CR2450N, 3V Lithium) with a cloth and push it into the battery holder. .Fig. 5‑52

    |

    • Do not touch the new battery with metal pliers, as it might be short-circuited and destroyed.

  2. Push the lid down until it clicks into place.

  3. Mount the controller (see “Replacing the Eckelmann controll-er”, pg. xxx).

  4. Go to HMI, run the program “Nozzle clean & height sensor calibration”. Additional work in case of data loss
