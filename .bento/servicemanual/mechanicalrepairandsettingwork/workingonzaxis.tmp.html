Replacing the motor and spindle of the Z axis drive

  • Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600.

  • Circlip plier.

  • Hook wrench 22-26 mm.

    1. Move the pallet out of the machine. Remove the motor

    2. Switch the machine’s main switch off and secure it against being switched on again.

    3. Remove the front cover of the cutting unit. .Front covers of the cutting unit Fig. 4‑7

    • Compressed air.

  2. Insert the spindle through the bearing mount.

  3. Guide two upper bearings to the spindle.

  4. Guide a sleeve to the spindle.

    |

  1. Tighten the jackscrew on the lock nut.

  2. Slightly knock the lower bearing from below to the spindle until the groove of spindle is visible.

  3. Fit circlip to the groove of spindle. .Fig. 4‑30

    |

  1. Mount the spray shield.

  2. Remove the fixture for ball nut and Z carriages.

  3. Install the Z slide:

    • Screw in 2x shoulder screws first, but not yet tighten.

    • Screw in 14x screws for carriages, but not yet tighten.

    • Screw in 4x screws for ball nut, but not yet tighten.

    • Move the Z slide up and down multiple times to be relieved of tension.

    • Tighten 2x shoulder screws by hand first, without Loctite.

    • Tighten 14x screws for carriages to 10 Nm, with Loctite.

    • Tighten 4x screws for ball nut to 4.5 Nm, with Loctite.

    • Do not use Loctite when assembling the shoulder screw. .Fig. 4‑31

      | |

|1 M5 screws (14x), carriages |2 M4 screws (4x), ball nut |3 Shoulder screws (2x) |

  1. Check if the Z slide move smoothly.

  2. Place the coupling.

  3. Using M3 screw, remove the flat key on the shaft of new motor. Install the new motor .Fig. 4‑32

    |

  1. Clean all the installation contact surface.

  2. Insert the motor into the YZ unit, aligning the slot of coupling to the key way.

    • The motor interface faces Y+ direction. .Fig. 4‑33

      |

  1. Tighten 2x screws on the coupling to 14 Nm.

  2. Rotate the motor to tighten the remaining 2 screws on the coupling to 14 Nm. .Fig. 4‑34

    |

  1. Rotate the motor back to original position.

  2. Tighten the screws of motor to 17 Nm.

  3. Perform the removal steps in the reverse order.

  4. Further work:

    • Reset error message E731.0 of the Inovance drive. (see “Reset error message E731.0 of Inovance drive”, pg. xxx)

    • Set the Z axis reference point offset value. (see “Checking and setting the Z axis reference point off-set value”, pg. xxx)

Replacing the guide rails

  • The machine’s main switch is switched off and secured against being switched on again.

  • The Z slide is removed (see “Replacing the motor and spindl-e of the Z axis drive”, pg. xxx). Means, Tools, Materials

  • 2x recirculating ball unit, consisting of 1 guide rail and 2 guid-e carriages, mat. no. 2750694.

  • Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600.

    1. Disassemble the spray shield under the Y slide. .Fig. 4‑35

      |

  1. Guide all guide carriages downwards from the rails.

  2. Remove the screw sealing caps from both guide rails.

  3. Dismantle both guide rails.

  4. Clean contact surfaces well and, if necessary, strip with an oil stone.

  5. Clean the new guide rail contact surface and, if necessary strip it with an oil stone.

  6. Insert the left guide rail into the Y slide. .Fig. 4‑36

    | |

|1 Sealing cap |2 Stop edge

  1. Screw in all screws with Loctite 243, but do not yet tighten.

  2. Fasten the guide rail:

    • Use a hammer handle to push the guide rail against the stop edge in the area of topmost screw.

    • Tighten the screw to 4.5 Nm.

  3. Repeat the procedure one after the other from top to bottom for all screws.

  4. Repeat steps 6. to 10. with the right guide rail. .Fig. 4‑37

    | |

|1 Sealing cap |2 Stop edge

  1. Insert all the screw sealing cap.

  2. Apply some grease to the guide rails before installing the guide carriages.

    • The Z guide carriages are maintenance free.

  3. Guide the new guide carriages onto the guide rail, with the milled surface facing left. .Fig. 4‑38

    |

|1 Milled surface

  1. Mount spray shield.

  2. Further work:

    • Mount Z slide. (see “Replacing the motor and spindle of Z axis drive”, pg. xxx)

    • Set the Z axis reference point offset value. (see “Checking and setting the Z axis reference point off-set value”, pg. xxx).

Checking and setting the Z axis reference point offset value

Condition * Z slide is at the lowest position. * All pallets are out of the machine. * Machine is switched on. * Any nozzle is set up. * Service user level in Vulcan system is active.

| a|

Homing before setting the Z axis reference point offset value. Risk of collision. * Roughly set the Z axis reference point offset value before homing. * Due to moving Z axis to 115 position, Z axis reference point offset value need to be set roughly in advance.

  1. Select “Configure” > “Machine Settings” on HMI. .Fig. 4‑39

    |

  1. Select “Axis Parameters” > “Z” axis.

  2. Scroll down and find “Referencing” catalog.

  3. Enter -195 in “Homing offset” and 1 in “Homing direction and sequence”. .Fig. 4‑40

    |

  1. Click “Close” and choose “Save and reload parameters” for the querying window.

  2. Go to “Setup” menu and carry out a reference run of Z axis. .Fig. 4‑41

    |

  1. Go back to the previous interface for setting Z referencing.

  2. Change “Homing direction and sequence” back to 0. .Fig. 4‑42

    |

  1. Click “Close” to save and reload parameters.

  2. In “Setup” interface, select “Homing” > “Automatic” and pressthe green START button to run homing. .Fig. 4‑43

    |

  1. In “Controls”, run “Go to park position” program. .Fig. 4‑44

    |

  1. Place a sheet metal at least 2 mm thick on the pallet.

  2. Move the pallet into the machine.

  3. Position cutting unit above the sheet metal.

  4. Move the cutting unit downwards slowly to approach the sheet metal.

    • Cutting unit goes down step by step with an increment of 0.5 mm, when it’s close to the sheet.

    • The cutting unit should nearly touch the sheet metal rather than colliding with the sheet. .Fig. 4‑45

      |

  1. Check the display value of Z axis on HMI. Nominal value: sheet thickness ± 0.5 mm.

  2. If not, select “Configure” > “Machine Settings” on HMI.

  3. Select “Axis Parameters” > “Z” axis.

  4. Scroll down and find “Referencing” catalog.

  5. Enter sheet thickness (e.g., 2 mm) in “Homing offset” and enter 1 in “Homing direction and sequence”. .Fig. 4‑46

    |

  1. Click “Close” and then “Save and reload parameters”

  2. Come back to “Setup” menu and carry out a reference run of Z axis. .Fig. 4‑47

    |

  1. Go back to the previous interface for setting Z referencing.

  2. Change “Homing direction and sequence” back to 0. .Fig. 4‑48

    |

  1. Click “Close” and then “Save and reload parameters”.

  2. Check the display value of Z axis on HMI. Nominal value: sheet thickness ± 0.5 mm.
