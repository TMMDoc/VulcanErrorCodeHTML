Replacing the Y axis motor and gearbox

  • The machine’s main switch is switched off and secured against being switched on again . Means, Tools, Materials

  • Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600.

  • Adjustable hook wrench 35-60 mm, mat. no. 2014343. Or hook wrench, mat. no.1655965.

  • Torque wrench, 20-120 Nm.

  • Torque wrench, 2.5-22.5 Nm.

  • If possible, disconnect coupling between engine and gearbox to remove both components separately.

Checking and setting the Y axis backlash

Condition * Machine is switched on and ready for operation. Means, Tools, Material * Standard tool set, mat. no. 0135600. * Dial gauge 1/1000 with magnetic support, mat. no. 0002419. * Bluetooth dial gauge, mat. no. 2369502. * Permanent marker. * Ruler or measuring tape.

  1. Release the bellows on the left and right of the Y slide and push them to the side.

  2. Find the marking for the highest point on Y rack. .Fig. 4‑64

    |

  1. Starting from this marking, measure 110 mm in the direction of Y- and make another marking there on the Y rack using a permanent marker.

  2. Push the Y slide until its right edge is flush with the new marking. .Fig. 4‑65

    |

    • Check the backlash either with the help of current limit key switch or using the Bluetooth dial gauge.

    • L99’s X/Y mobile fixed stop is different from PLL’s. .Fig. 4‑68

    |

  1. Set the key switch for the motor current limit to right (ON). .Fig. 4‑70

  1. Switch on drives and open the safety door.

  2. Acknowledge the service mode is active at the bottom-left of HMI. .Fig. 4‑71

    |

  1. Acknowledge the feedrate changed to 0 by PLC controller.

  2. Position the dial gauge against Y slide and zero. .Fig. 4‑72

    |

  1. Push the Y slide in the + and – directions with a force of about 3 kg and read off the backlash from the dial gauge. Set value: 0.01±0.005 mm.

  2. Position the Bluetooth dial gauge to the right of the Y slide and zero. Check the backlash with the Bluetooth dial gauge .Fig. 4‑73

    |

  1. Close the safety door and drive on.

  2. In HMI, select “Setup” > “settings”.

  3. Enter 0.001 under all the “Step” column. .Fig. 4‑74

    |

    • The “Step” value must be reset at the end.

  4. In jog mode, press “-Y” arrow button to move in the minus direction until the dial gauge deflects.

  5. In jog mode, press “+Y” arrow button to move in the plus direction and count the number of steps until the dial gauge display changes continuously.

    • The number of steps x 0.001mm equals the backlash.

    • Set value: 0.01±0.005 mm.

  6. Reset the step increment back to original value.

  7. Loosen 4x screws on the gearbox flange mount. Set the Y axis backlash .Fig. 4‑75

    |

|1 Long screws (2x) |2 Screws with washers (4x)

  1. Adjust the backlash with the help of wedge mechanism:

    • To reduce the backlash, screw in the 2x long screws by the same distance.

    • To increase the backlash, unscrew the 2x long screws bythe same distance and then press the gearbox against it.

  2. Tighten 4x screws on the gearbox flange mount to 30 Nm.

  3. Check the backlash again.

  4. Fit the bellow supporting bar and bellow mounting bracket.

  5. Attach the bellows on the left and on the right of the Y axis.

  6. Mount the covers of the cutting unit.

  7. Mount the side enclosure.

  8. Further work:

    • Check and set the Y axis reference point offset value andfixed stops of the Y axis. (see “Checking and setting the Y axis reference point off-set value”, pg. xxx)

    • Set the Y axis limit switch. (see “Checking and setting the Y axis limit switch”, pg. xxx)

Replacing Y guide carriage

  • The machine’s main switch is switched off and secured agai-nst being switched on again.

  • The cutting unit has been removed and is lying on the motionunit (see “Replacing the motor and spindle of the Z axis drive”, pg. xxx). Means, Tools, Materials

  • Feeler gauge 0.02mm

  • Gloves.

    1. Position the YZ unit roughly in the middle. Replace Y guide carriage

    2. Remove the stop at the end of the guide rail on both side. .Remove Y axis stops Fig. 4‑76

      | |

|1 Screws (3x), 14 Nm |2 Screws (2x), 36 Nm

  1. Move the Z slide upwards to loosen all the screws from the Yaxis lower guide carriages. .Fig. 4‑77

    |

  1. Move the Z slide downwards to loosen all the screws from the Y axis upper guide carriages. .Fig. 4‑78

    |

  1. Disconnect the lubrication line.

  2. Remove the lower grease collection plate. .Fig. 4‑79

    | |

|1 Lubrication line, lower |2 Grease collection plate, lower

  1. If necessary, remove the upper grease collection plate. .Fig. 4‑80

    | |

|1 Lubrication line, upper |2 Grease collection plate, upper

  1. Remove the screws of one guide carriage.

  2. Pull out the target guide carriage.

    • If necessary, remove the Y axis gearbox which will make the replacement work easier. (see “Replacing the Y axis motor and gearbox”, pg. xxx).

  3. Slide the guide carriage to the left or right off the rail.

  4. Clean all contact surfaces and, if necessary, strip with oil stone.

    • Do not spray the cleaning agent directly onto the guide rail, but onto a cloth.

  5. Remove the grease nipple (mat. no. 2750651) from the old guide carriage and install the grease nipple on the new one. .Fig. 4‑81

    |

  1. Lubricate the new guide carriage before installation. (see “Lubricating the new guide carriage before installation”, pg. xxx)

  2. Insert the new, lubricated guide carriage onto the guide rails from the left or right using an insertion aid with the milled surface facing upwards.

    • When inserting the guide carriage, use an insertion aid to prevent the balls from falling out. .Insertion aid (example) Fig. 4‑82

      |

  1. Position the carriage behind the Y slide.

  2. Screw in the screws with washers but do not yet tighten. .Fig. 4‑83

    |

  1. Repeat the procedure from step 8 to step 16 to replace other three Y guide carriages.

  2. Slightly tap the YZ unit downwards to make the YZ unit touchthe Y axis upper guide carriage’s milled surface.

  3. Tighten all the screws to 36 Nm.

    • Tighten the screws diagonally.

  4. Check that the Y slide touches the upper carriage’s milled surface with the help of a feeler gauge. Clearance < 0.02 mm. .Fig. 4‑84

    |

  1. Further work:

    • Perform the removal steps in the reverse order.

    • Install the cutting unit (see “Replacing the motor and spi-ndle of the Z axis drive” pg. xxx).

    • Check and set the Y axis reference point offset value andfixed stops of the Y axis (see “Checking and setting the Yaxis reference point offset value”, pg. xxx).

    • Check the Y axis limit switch (see “Checking and setting the Y axis limit switch”, pg. xxx).

    • S02 machine: Right limit switch: -38 mm. Left limit switch: 1526 mm.
